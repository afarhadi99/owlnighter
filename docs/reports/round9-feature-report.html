Feature & Verification Report

owlnighter

A Duolingo-style nightly reading habit app — every feature below was rebuilt, installed, and driven by hand on a live Android emulator, not just compiled and assumed to work.

This report walks through owlnighter end to end: the core nightly reading loop, and the two most recent rounds of work — real push notifications with a Duolingo-style home-screen widget, and a mood-reactive owl mascot paired with an app-wide header audit. Every screenshot below was captured live on-device during verification, in the order the feature actually happens.

The nightly reading loop

Verified live

The spine of the app: add a book, get a nightly reading path generated from it, read tonight's assignment, take a short comprehension quiz with instant per-question feedback, then land on a full celebration screen that updates your streak. Every step below was actually driven by hand — tapped, typed, and submitted against the real API — not just described.

The mood-reactive owl

New this round

The owl mascot now visibly reacts to one thing: did you do tonight's reading yet, and how late is it? A shared helper reuses the same day / evening / night boundaries the home-screen widget already used, so the in-app owl and the widget owl always agree with each other.

Here is exactly what the owl draws for each mood — rendered straight from the same path data and coordinates as the real _OwlPainter, not a mockup:

On verifying the angry/worried states live: reaching them on-device would mean rolling the emulator's clock forward into a fresh, unread day — Android refuses to set the system clock without root on this build. The renders above are pulled directly from _OwlPainter's own coordinates (eyebrow lines, squint radius, eyelid rects), not redrawn by eye, and both moods are additionally covered by a dedicated widget test that renders the banner with a fixed night-time clock and asserts the owl comes up in exactly its angry pose.

Real push notifications

Verified live

A live Settings toggle schedules an honest-to-goodness local notification — not a mock. Turning it on made Android schedule a real recurring alarm on the device, confirmed by reading it straight out of the OS's own alarm manager.

Home-screen widget

New this round

A Duolingo-style widget, placed on the actual Android home screen — own brand art throughout, never Duolingo's. It changes background, copy, and now the owl's face across four states: neutral by day, worried by evening, angry once night falls and the streak is on the line, and — pictured here — happy with a touch of blush once tonight's reading is actually done.
